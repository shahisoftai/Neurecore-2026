## FINAL AUDIT REPORT

### Executive Summary

The Emergency Nutrition Programme for the Ministry of Health successfully completed its 2-month mandate. The programme screened 284,600 children across 4 districts, identified and treated 467 SAM cases with a 99.6% recovery rate, and transitioned all operations to the Health Department. The programme finished 4.9% under budget (USD 42,000 surplus) and received positive evaluations from all donor partners.

### Programme Outcome: SUCCESS

### Goal Achievement

| Goal | Achievement | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Screen 288,000 children | 284,600 screened | 98.8% ✓ |
| Identify and treat all SAM cases | 467 identified, 465 recovered | 99.6% ✓ |
| Reduce SAM mortality below 5% | 0.43% (2 deaths) | 99.1% ✓ |
| Reach 18,000 children U5 | 18,450 reached | 102.5% ✓ |
| Reach 6,000 PLW | 6,120 reached | 102% ✓ |
| Complete on schedule | 60 days exactly | 100% ✓ |
| Remain within budget | USD 808K of USD 850K | 95.1% ✓ |

|---|---|---|---|

| Supplies | 340,000 | 332,000 | -2.4% |

**Variance Analysis:** The programme came in 4.9% under budget. The contingency fund was only partially used due to effective risk management. The slight overspend in training was due to the District C surge response, which was offset by savings in other categories.

### Timeline Variance

The programme completed exactly on schedule (60 days). No timeline extensions were required despite:
- Security incident in District D (Day 3)
- Weather delays in District C (Days 6, 9, 16, 18)
- Supply chain delays (Day 3, Day 16)
- Fraud incident (Day 33)

Contingency planning effectively mitigated all delays.
